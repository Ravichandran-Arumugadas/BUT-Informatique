CROWDS 2023

Discover the fascinating world of crowds at the Cité des Sciences,
a captivating exhibition that explores collective dynamics and the societal impact of these phenomena.

    GALLERY

Admission Prices:

Regular: €13 | Reduced: €10
Ages 10 and above
Exhibition in French, English, and Spanish

Opening Hours:

Tuesday to Sunday
From 10am to 6pm
October 18, 2022, to January 7, 2024

Address:

30 Avenue Corentin Cariou,
75019 Paris
Metro: Line 7, Tramway: T3b

On the program:

    9:00 am - 10:00 am: Opening of the "Crowds" Exhibition

  • Visitors are invited to freely explore the exhibition and discover the various interactive installations and multimedia displays.

    • 10:00 am - 11:30 am: Conference: "Understanding Crowd Dynamics"

  • Experts will share their knowledge on collective behavior, crowd movements, and the impact of crowds on our modern society.

    • 11:30 am - 1:00 pm: Interactive Workshops: "Crowd Management"

  • Visitors will have the opportunity to participate in hands-on workshops where they can experience the challenges and opportunities of crowd management. They can step into the shoes of event organizers and explore the technologies used to predict and manage crowd movements.

    • 1:00 pm - 2:30 pm: Lunch Break

  • Visitors will have time to have lunch and relax.

    • 2:30 pm - 4:00 pm: Documentary Screening: "Crowds Around the World"

  • Fascinating documentaries will be screened, highlighting the various manifestations of crowds in everyday life around the world. Visitors will have the opportunity to discover gatherings at sports events, political demonstrations, concerts, and traffic flows in major cities.

    • 4:00 pm - 5:00 pm: Interactive Concert: "Harmony of Crowds"

  • A talented group of musicians will invite visitors to participate in an interactive concert where they can sing and play music together. A unique immersive experience to create connections and share musical passion.

    • 5:00 pm - 6:00 pm: Closure of the "Crowds" Exhibition

  • Last chance for visitors to enjoy the exhibition before its closure.
